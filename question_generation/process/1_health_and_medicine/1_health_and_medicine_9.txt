VIII. Heavy/Critical Early Warning Indicators
(i) Adults.
Changes in the following indicators should alert for deterioration.
1. Progressive worsening of hypoxemia or respiratory distress.
2. deterioration of tissue oxygenation indicators (e.g., oxygen saturation, oxygenation index) or progressive elevation of lactate.
3. progressive decrease in peripheral blood lymphocyte count or progressive increase in inflammatory factors such as interleukin 6 (IL-6), CRP, ferritin, etc.
4. Significant elevation of coagulation-related indicators such as D-dimer.
