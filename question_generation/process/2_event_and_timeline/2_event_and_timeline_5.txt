On March 4, 2020, the outbreak spread rapidly in Italy and the Italian government decided to close all schools across the country for two weeks from now. It also closed all other stores except pharmacies and supermarkets. As of March 3, 2020, there were 2,036 confirmed cases throughout Italy, of which 83 people recovered and were discharged from the hospital.
On the same day, the first confirmed cases of 2019 coronavirus disease were reported in Hungary, Poland and Slovenia.
On March 5, 2020, Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe announced that all passengers entering Japan from China and South Korea will be sent to a designated quarantine facility for 14 days starting at 00:00 a.m. on March 9, 2020, and will not be allowed to travel on public transportation.
On the same day, the first confirmed cases of 2019 coronavirus disease were reported in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Palestine and South Africa.
