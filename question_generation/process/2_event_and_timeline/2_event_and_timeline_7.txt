On March 12, 2020, Mi Feng, spokesperson of the National Health and Wellness Commission of the People's Republic of China, said that 15 new confirmed cases (including 8 cases in Wuhan and 6 cases imported from abroad) were diagnosed in mainland China on March 11, 2020, and the number of new cases in Wuhan dropped to single digits. The above information suggests that the peak of the current epidemic in mainland China has generally passed, the number of new cases is continuing to decline, and the epidemic overall remains at a low level.

On the evening of March 14, 2020, the President of the Spanish government (Prime Minister), José Sánchez, announced a nationwide "city closure" for 15 days to contain the Newcastle pneumonia outbreak, prohibiting all people and private vehicles from going outside except for work, food and medicine purchases, medical treatment and other essential and emergency situations. At the same time, all public places except supermarkets, pharmacies, barber stores and other essential products and services were closed. Schools nationwide were closed and replaced with online classes, and passenger transportation capacity was reduced. The same day Portuguese Prime Minister Costa announced that schools would be closed. This initiative, which will last until April 9, 2020, will affect 1.6 million students; in Belgium, in addition to school closures, only stores providing essential services will remain open from March 14, 2020.
On March 14, 2020, Polish Prime Minister Morawiecki said that Poland would close its borders for 10 days, and that the Polish government would allow only domestic lines to continue to operate in terms of rail and air traffic.
