At 2200 hours on January 19, 2020, the Wuhan Municipal Health Commission declared that confirmed cases were found among close contacts of confirmed cases, i.e., evidence of human-to-human transmission was found. To prevent further spread of COVID-19 from Wuhan, the center of the outbreak, all traffic in and out of Wuhan was banned from 10:00 a.m. on January 23, 2020 2020, and a day later, the entire Hubei province. In terms of the population involved, this appears to be the largest quarantine (movement restriction) event in human history.

During the two weeks from January 31, 2020 to February 12, 2020, a number of cities outside of Hubei Province in China announced the implementation of closed administration. According to incomplete statistics, there are at least 9 primary administrative regions (4 municipalities, 4 provinces, and 1 autonomous region, for a total of 156 secondary administrative regions) with at least 51 secondary administrative regions in other provinces and cities, for a total of 207 (including 26 provincial capitals and sub-provincial cities); among the cities upgraded to fully closed wartime control, there are 2 secondary administrative regions.
On February 1, 2020, an 80-year-old man from Hong Kong was diagnosed with the infection, having boarded the Diamond Princess in Yokohama, Japan, on January 20, 2020, and disembarked at the Kai Tak Cruise Terminal in Hong Kong on January 25, 2020. After the incident, the cruise ship stayed at the port of Yokohama at Daikokubutou from February 4, 2020, and the personnel on board were quarantined in their individual cabins for 14 days before those with relevant symptoms were quarantined by the Japanese Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare. As of March 2, 2020, 705 people on board have been confirmed, including 6 who died after disembarking.
On February 2, 2020, China's National Health Construction Commission notified a cumulative total of 17,205 confirmed cases and 361 deaths in mainland China, with the number of deaths surpassing the total number of deaths in mainland China from SARS.
