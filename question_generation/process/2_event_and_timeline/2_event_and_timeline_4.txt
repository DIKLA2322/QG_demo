On February 26, 2020, WHO Director General Tan Desai said in Geneva that the number of new cases reported outside of China exceeded that of China for the first time on February 25, and that the new outbreak in China has reached a peak "inflection point" and that there are now concerns about global spread, with the number of new cases still rising in Italy and Iran. At the same time, cases exported to neighboring countries have begun to be detected, and the outbreak has spread to all continents inhabited by humans.
On February 27, 2020, as the epidemic continues to intensify in Japan and in order to prevent the further spread of New Crown Pneumonia in Japan, Prime Minister (PM) Shinzo Abe made a request to suspend classes at elementary, junior high and high schools nationwide at a meeting of the Government Countermeasures Headquarters held on the afternoon of the 27th. From March 2, 2020, public elementary, junior high and high schools nationwide will be temporarily closed for the holiday until the end of spring break. The government has also issued a circular to sports and cultural organizations to suspend all major events and public performances until March 15, 2020.
On February 29, 2020, World Health Organization (WHO) Director General Ted Tam announced in Geneva that the global risk level for the outbreak had been raised from "high" to "very high". The first death from 2019 coronavirus disease is reported in the United States.
